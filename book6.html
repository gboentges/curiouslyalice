The Dodo Knight

Rating: 4/5

This is such a delightful little book. It's about the time that Charles Dodgson got to spend with the Liddell's through Alice's POV. It also adresses the possibility of what could have happened between him and her family in a more accurate, believable way. You can tell the author really cares about this subject because she does a really good job in portraying Charles' charismatic and quirky personality. I also enjoyed Alice's personality, how she is aware and smart for her age. Besides very few inaccuracies, it is pretty spot on his life. I even learned new things about him! I wish there had been illustrations in it, but it is not detrimental to the story. I do think that it was a little short, and some of the most important scenes, like the famous golden afternoon, were a little rushed. In the end though, it's a book that I enjoyed tremendously, and would recommend to all Carrollian lovers.
